steps:
  - Cube chicken. Whisk gochujang, soy, brown sugar, sriracha, rice vinegar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and honey into a marinade. Toss chicken in it and rest 20-30 min (no longer; the vinegar softens diced meat past an hour).
  - Bring chicken + marinade up in a hot pot. Cook through to 165F (the marinade poaches it; this is a braise-glaze, not a sear).
  - Reduce hard, uncovered, over high heat until the liquid tightens to a glaze that coats the chicken.
  - Whisk the cornstarch slurry cold and add half; bubble 30-60 sec. Add the rest only if still thin. Stop once glossy; over-reduced glaze goes dry/sticky.
  - Separately, stir-fry the frozen vegetables in a screaming-hot pan (in batches if over ~1 lb) until the water cooks off and they take color. Salt lightly.
  - Fold chicken and veg together to reunite, 1-2 min. Finish with scallion and sesame seed. If tight, loosen with a splash of water.

## Origin

Started as a stir-fry, became a braise-glaze after the sauce got repurposed as a marinade.
Turned out better for it: deeper flavor, forgiving method. Keep the veg cooked separately so
they don't dump water into the glaze.
