steps:
  - "Make the caramel: in a wide skillet, melt the brown sugar with 1 tbsp water over medium heat, swirling (don't stir), until it darkens to amber, ~3-4 min. Watch closely; it goes from caramel to burnt fast."
  - Bloom aromatics. Add the oil, shallot, garlic, and ginger off the direct heat (it'll sputter), stir 30-60 sec until fragrant.
  - "Build the braise: add fish sauce, the 1/2 cup water, black pepper, and chili. Bring to a simmer; it should smell sharp then mellow."
  - Simmer the salmon. Nestle the fillets skin-down, spoon sauce over, cover, and simmer gently 8-10 min, basting once or twice, until just cooked through and glazed.
  - Finish. Off heat, add the lime juice and scallion. Taste, it should be savory-sweet-funky with a bright edge. Serve over rice, spooning the caramel over everything.

## Origin

Targets the tangy-funky, fish-sauce-vinegar flavor you named as your deepest craving. Vietnamese
caramel (kho) is the canonical home of that savory-sweet-funky profile: fish sauce reduced with
caramel into something glossy and deep. Salmon makes it fast and hits the omega-3 gap at once.
