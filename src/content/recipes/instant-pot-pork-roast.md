steps:
  - Mix all the dry-rub spices (brown sugar, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, cinnamon, thyme, pepper) in a bowl, breaking up lumps. Rub all over the pork, covering every inch.
  - Set the Instant Pot to saute, add 2 tbsp olive oil. When fragrant, cook the onion and minced garlic 2-3 min; remove.
  - Add the last 2 tbsp oil and sear the pork 2-3 min per side to brown. Remove.
  - "Deglaze: pour the wine and broth in, scraping up all the brown bits (prevents the burn warning)."
  - Return the onion and garlic, add rosemary, set the trivet, place the pork on top. Seal and cook manual high pressure 70 min. Add carrots and potatoes for the last portion (or natural-release then pressure-cook the veg briefly).
  - "Make the sauce: strain some cooking liquid, stir in the balsamic and a cornstarch slurry, simmer to thicken. Shred the pork and serve with the veg and sauce."

## Origin

The classic no-fuss pressure-cooker roast: spice rub, sear, braise, that turns pork shoulder
into a fall-apart one-pot meal. Great for a lazy Sunday and days of leftovers.
